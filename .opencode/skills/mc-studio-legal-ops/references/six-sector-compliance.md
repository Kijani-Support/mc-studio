# The Six Sita Sector Industries — Compliance Lens

The Sita Sector Program prioritizes six sectors. Each has different weight on
compliance, data privacy, and IP handling in the Legal Health Check. This file is a **starting
checklist of areas to verify**, not exhaustive law — regulators, thresholds, and filing
requirements change, so confirm current status (e.g. via web search) before advising on
specifics like fees or deadlines.

## 1. Fintech
- **Key regulators/areas to check:** Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) licensing for payment/lending
  products, Competition Authority of Kenya (CAK), Data Protection Act (DPA) — especially
  cross-border data flows and consumer credit data.
- **Legal Health Check emphasis:** licensing & permits (Section 1), data protection registration
  & DPIA (Section 4), commercial SLAs with banking/payment partners (Section 3).
- **Typical tier signal:** licensing complexity often pushes fintech startups toward GROW even
  at modest headcount.

## 2. Healthtech
- **Key regulators/areas to check:** Ministry of Health, Pharmacy and Poisons Board (where
  applicable), Data Protection Act (health data is sensitive personal data under Kenyan law —
  higher DPIA priority), patient-consent frameworks.
- **Legal Health Check emphasis:** Section 4 (Privacy & DPIA) is usually the top-priority
  section; IP (Section 5) matters if proprietary diagnostics/algorithms are involved.

## 3. Edtech
- **Key regulators/areas to check:** Ministry of Education guidance where curricula/content are
  involved, Data Protection Act with specific attention to **minors' data** (parental
  consent, data minimization for under-18 users), content licensing/IP.
- **Legal Health Check emphasis:** Section 4 (privacy, especially minors) and Section 5 (IP —
  content licensing, copyright on learning materials).

## 4. Agritech
- **Key regulators/areas to check:** Kenya Plant Health Inspectorate Service (KEPHIS), Agriculture
  and Food Authority (AFA) where produce/inputs are regulated, land-use/lease considerations for
  physical operations, export compliance if cross-border (EAC).
- **Legal Health Check emphasis:** Section 1 (licenses/permits), Section 3 (supplier/off-taker
  contracts, SLAs), cross-border scaling questions especially relevant given EAC trade.

## 5. Retail & Ecommerce
- **Key regulators/areas to check:** Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) — VAT/digital service tax
  exposure, Competition Authority of Kenya (consumer protection), Data Protection Act (customer
  data at scale), e-commerce-specific consumer protection rules.
- **Legal Health Check emphasis:** Section 3 (SOPs, SLAs, contract review at higher transaction
  volume) and Section 4 (data protection at consumer scale).

## 6. Energy & Manufacturing
- **Key regulators/areas to check:** Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA), National
  Environment Management Authority (NEMA) for environmental compliance, Kenya Bureau of
  Standards (KEBS) for product standards, land/lease and property purchase agreements (relevant
  to the GROW-tier lease clause: agreements under/over KSh 20,000,000).
- **Legal Health Check emphasis:** Section 1 (licenses/permits, formal structure) and
  commercial contracts (Section 3) tied to supply chain and equipment agreements.

## Using this file
When a startup's sector is identified, pull the relevant section above and cross-reference it
against the Legal Health Check gaps (`legal-health-check.md`) to prioritize which non-compliant
items matter most for that sector — rather than treating all checklist gaps as equally urgent.
